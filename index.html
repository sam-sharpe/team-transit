New York on lockdown, New Yorkers on the move
Interactive stories by Elias Guerra, Caroline Leddy, Hafsa Quraishi, Jill Shah and Sam Sharpe.

New Yorkers never stop – most of them at least. Even with the city on lockdown due to the COVID-19 pandemic, essential workers living in some of New York City’s lowest-income neighborhoods continued commuting through abnormally busy subway stations. Others, especially healthcare workers, altered their normal commutes to use Citibikes. But for New Yorkers with disabilities, forced to contend with a dearth of accessible subway stations, abnormal commutes are nothing new.
